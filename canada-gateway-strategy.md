# Canada as a Gateway: Flying Asia -> Canada -> USA to Save on Airfare

## Research Date: March 16, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Flying from Asia to Canada (especially Vancouver) and then taking a separate cheap flight to the USA **can** be cheaper than flying direct to US cities, but the savings depend heavily on timing, airline, and specific routes. The strategy works best when:

1. You can find a deal on Asia-to-Vancouver flights (often cheaper than Asia-to-LAX/SFO)
2. You combine it with a budget carrier like Flair Airlines for the Canada-to-USA leg
3. You are flexible with dates and willing to accept the risks of separate tickets

---

## 1. Asia to Canada: Key Routes and Pricing

### Vancouver (YVR) - The Best Gateway

Vancouver is geographically closest to Asia and consistently offers the cheapest transpacific fares from Canada.

| Route | Airline | Approx. Round-Trip Price | Notes |
|-------|---------|--------------------------|-------|
| Tokyo (NRT) - Vancouver | ZIPAIR | C$622 - C$742 | Budget long-haul; 4x weekly; one-way under $400 |
| Tokyo - Vancouver | Various | ~US$491+ RT | Multiple carriers |
| Beijing - Vancouver | Air China | ~US$852+ RT | 10 weekly non-stop flights |
| Seoul - Vancouver | Various | ~US$574+ RT | |
| Hong Kong - Vancouver | Cathay Pacific | ~C$699+ RT | Some of the best deals from HK |
| Shanghai - Vancouver | China Eastern | TBD (route resuming Sept 2025) | New service adds competition |
| Shenzhen - Vancouver | Hainan Airlines | ~US$433 one-way | Via Airpaz deals |

**Key finding**: Vancouver-Asia round trips can be found for C$700-1,000 during off-peak (September-October, January-February). ZIPAIR is the price leader for the Tokyo route.

### Toronto (YYZ) - More Expensive but More US Connections

- Toronto-Asia round trips average C$1,361+ (cheapest month: September)
- China Eastern operates 33 flights/week from Toronto (Shanghai route, 4x weekly)
- Generally C$300-500 more expensive than Vancouver for Asia routes

### Example: Hong Kong to Los Angeles

| Strategy | Approximate Cost | Notes |

### Verdict on Pricing

The Canada gateway strategy **does NOT automatically save money** on a pure price basis for most routes. Direct Asia-to-USA flights on competitive routes (Tokyo-LAX, Seoul-LAX) are often comparable or cheaper.

**Where it DOES make sense:**
- When there's a specific sale on the Asia-Vancouver leg (ZIPAIR, Hainan, etc.)
- When you actually want to visit Canada for a few days (making the "extra" flight a feature, not a bug)
- For destinations where direct Asia-USA flights are expensive or unavailable
- When combining with Air Canada's stopover program (see below)

## 4. Free Stopover Programs

### Air Canada Toronto Stopover Program
- **Duration**: Up to 48 hours (some sources say up to 7 days for US-originating passengers)
- **Cost**: No additional airfare cost
- **Eligible routes**: Passengers traveling between the US/Canada and Europe or Asia via Toronto
- **How to book**: Use Air Canada's multi-city booking page
- **Caveat**: You arrange your own lodging

### Air Canada Stopover via Vancouver/Montreal
- Air Canada offers stopovers of up to 24 hours in Toronto, Montreal, or Vancouver for passengers traveling between the US and international destinations

### Aeroplan (Air Canada Loyalty Program) Stopovers
- Book a stopover for only 5,000 extra Aeroplan points per direction
- Stopover can be up to **45 days**
- Available on award bookings worldwide
- **Important limitation**: Cannot build a stopover in Canada or the US on award tickets

## 5. Airline-Specific Intelligence

### ZIPAIR (Tokyo-Vancouver)
- Budget long-haul carrier (JAL subsidiary)
- Vancouver route: 4x weekly (Mon, Wed, Fri, Sat)
- One-way from ~$324 CAD; round trip from ~$622 CAD
- Book direct on zipair.net (cheaper than third-party sites)
- Best prices: January-February, September-October
- **Best budget option for Tokyo-Vancouver**

### Cathay Pacific (Hong Kong-Vancouver/Toronto)
- Premium carrier with frequent sales
- Hong Kong to Canada from ~C$699 RT
- Current promo: CACN40OFF code for China-Canada discount
- Business class from ~US$4,494 RT

### Air China (Beijing-Vancouver)
- 10 weekly non-stop flights Beijing-Vancouver
- Round trip from ~US$852
- Book on Sundays for 6-13% savings vs. Friday bookings
- Book 2 months in advance for ~10% savings

### Hainan Airlines (Various China cities-Vancouver)
- Routes: Vancouver to Shenzhen, Shanghai, Beijing
- One-way deals spotted as low as US$433 (Airpaz)
- Cheapest month: April

### China Eastern (Shanghai-Vancouver/Toronto)
- Shanghai-Vancouver route resumed Sept 2025
- Toronto-Shanghai: 4x weekly
- Adding capacity = more competition = potentially lower fares

### Flair Airlines (Canada domestic / Canada-USA)
- Ultra-low-cost carrier based in Canada
- Vancouver-LA from C$35 one-way (sale) / ~C$77-137 normally
- Use promo codes (e.g., 26FOR2026 for 26% off)
- **The key enabler for the Canada gateway strategy**

### WestJet (Canada domestic / Canada-USA)
- Vancouver-LA from ~C$104 one-way
- Vancouver-Seattle from ~C$104 one-way
- More reliable than Flair, slightly higher prices

## 6. Multi-City Itinerary Ideas

### Option A: Asia -> Vancouver (stay 2-3 days) -> LA -> Asia
- Book: Asia-Vancouver RT on one ticket + Vancouver-LA RT on separate Flair ticket
- Example: ZIPAIR Tokyo-Vancouver RT ($622) + Flair Vancouver-LA RT ($137) = ~$759 total
- You get a Vancouver visit included

### Option B: Open-Jaw via Canada
- Book: Asia -> Vancouver (one-way) + Los Angeles -> Asia (one-way)
- Take Flair/WestJet Vancouver -> LA in between
- Sometimes cheaper than round trips; use Google Flights multi-city search

### Option C: Air Canada with Toronto Stopover
- Book: Asia -> Toronto (stopover 48 hrs) -> US destination on single Air Canada ticket
- No extra airfare for the Toronto visit
- Works well for US East Coast destinations

### Option D: Separate positioning via Seattle
- If your US destination is Seattle/Portland, just take a bus or train from Vancouver
- BoltBus/FlixBus Vancouver-Seattle: ~$20-40 USD, 4 hours
- Amtrak Cascades Vancouver-Seattle: ~$40-60 USD, 4 hours
- Avoids a separate flight entirely

## 7. Risks and Caveats

### Separate Tickets Risk
- **No through-checked baggage**: You must collect and re-check bags at the Canadian airport
- **No missed connection protection**: If your Asia flight is delayed and you miss the Canada-USA flight, you lose that ticket
- **Double security/customs**: You clear Canadian customs/immigration, then US preclearance
- **Time buffer needed**: Allow at least 4-6 hours between flights on separate tickets, or better yet, overnight

### Canadian Entry Requirements
- Most nationalities need an eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) for Canada: C$7
- Some nationalities need a full visa
- Make sure your passport/visa situation allows transit or visit in Canada

### Currency Considerations
- Canadian dollar is typically worth less than USD (~0.72-0.75 USD per CAD)
- Prices listed in CAD are roughly 25-28% less in USD terms
- This makes Canadian-departure flights look even cheaper in USD

### The Flytrippers Finding
- Air Canada charges Canadians significantly more than Americans for identical flights
- Example: Toronto-Tokyo RT was $1,528 from Toronto but only $848 when booked with a US connection (via Columbus) - a 44% difference
- This suggests Americans booking Asia flights via Canada can sometimes find bargains, while Canadians may benefit from originating in US border cities

---

## 8. Bottom Line Recommendations

1. **Best pure budget play**: ZIPAIR Tokyo-Vancouver + Flair Vancouver-LA. Total can be under $500 RT during sales.

2. **Best for Hong Kong travelers**: Watch for Cathay Pacific sales to Vancouver (C$699 RT), then add Flair to your US destination.

3. **Best if you want a Canada visit**: Book this as a feature - spend 2-3 days in Vancouver, then fly budget to the US. The "premium" over a direct flight is often just $100-200 and you get a bonus destination.

4. **Best for US East Coast**: Use Air Canada's Toronto stopover program - free 48-hour stop in Toronto on your way from Asia to US East Coast cities.

5. **Best for Seattle/Portland**: Fly to Vancouver, take the bus/train. Saves the most money.

6. **Skip this strategy for**: Beijing to USA (Air China direct is usually cheaper), or any route where direct competition keeps prices low.

7. **Always compare**: Use Google Flights multi-city search to compare the split-ticket Canada strategy against direct flights before committing.
