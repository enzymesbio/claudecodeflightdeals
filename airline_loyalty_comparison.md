## 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

### If lowest total cost matters most:
**Air Premia wins on price** ($601/pp) despite earning nearly worthless points. The $227/pp savings over Korean Air dwarfs any mileage value difference.

### If you value earning toward free flights:
**Korean Air or Asiana** are best. A family of 3 earns ~35,760 miles on a single RT -- nearly half of a free RT ticket. After 2-3 paid round trips, the family has enough pooled miles for a free ticket.

### If you have US credit cards that earn transferable points:
- **ANA** (Amex MR 1:1) or **Cathay** (Amex/Cap1/Citi 1:1) are valuable because you can top up earned miles with credit card points to reach award thresholds faster.
- **Korean Air** is harder to earn from credit cards in the US (no direct transfer partner).

### If family pooling is critical:
**Asiana** (up to 8 members, no fee, works on Star Alliance) > **Korean Air** (up to 5, works on KE and non-SkyTeam) > **ANA** (requires international flight in past 3 years) > **United** (pooled miles only for United flights)

### Asiana Club sunset warning:
Korean Air completed its acquisition of Asiana. The Asiana brand and Asiana Club are expected to be merged into Korean Air SKYPASS by end of 2026. Current Asiana Club members should track how their miles will be converted.

## 6. NOTES ON DATA QUALITY

- Ticket prices are estimates based on web searches and will vary significantly by date, season, and booking timing.
- Air Premia's points system is revenue-based in KRW, making direct comparison to distance-based programs approximate.
- United and Delta use revenue-based earning, which penalizes budget fares but rewards expensive tickets.
- Korean Air's own award chart for economy Korea-Americas is peak/off-peak variable; the 80,000 figure is for SkyTeam partner awards.
- All mile values are based on typical economy redemptions; premium cabin redemptions can yield 2-5x higher value per mile.
- Cathay Pacific earning figures reflect post-August 2025 rates.
